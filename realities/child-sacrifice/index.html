Child sacrifice has a long history spanning many continents and cultures.

Currently, child sacrifice is most common in Africa, specifically in Uganda.

Why are children being sacrificed?

In Uganda, witch doctors advertise their ability to communicate with spirits.

Clients will hire them for any number of reasons, such as a wish for better health or for economic prosperity.

Traditionally, a sacrifice is required, and usually this would consist of farm animals like chickens.

When this fails to resolve the situation, human sacrifices are required (according to the witch doctors).

What is a child sacrifice?

The witch doctor begins by abducting a small child, preferring children that have not been injured or have had their ears pierced, amongst other things (they desire "pure" children).

After abducting the child, the witch doctor and their subordinates will restrain the child.

While the child is fully aware, they will remove the child's blood, organs, eyes, head, and genitals.

From there, they will often use these "materials" for potions or medicines.

Alternatively, the child will be buried dead or alive in a new building constuction to bring good luck to the building.

Why does this still happen?

Money. Power.

Witch doctors promise great wealth and prosperity in exchange for their services.

With the child sacrifice industry boom in Uganda during the early 21st century, performing child sacrifices became a more lucrative position.

Additionally, many government officials now believe that they must perform a child sacrifice in order to win an election.

The beliefs persist due to financial gain, as well as the witch doctors' claims that child sacrifice is part of the country's traditional medicine.

However, Ugandan traditional healers do not use flesh or human sacrifices for healing.

Despite legal battles and new bills against child sacrifice, there are still around 3,000 Ugandan children abducted annually.
